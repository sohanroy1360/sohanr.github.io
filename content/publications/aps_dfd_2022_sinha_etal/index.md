abstract: "In this study, the thrust performance of a 2D elliptic symmetrical flapping airfoil at very high flapping frequencies is investigated. We implement asymmetric flapping by using different frequencies for the downstroke and upstroke. Results show that asymmetric configurations yield improved time-averaged thrust and non-zero lift compared to symmetric strokes in the thrust-degrading frequency range. We further analyze transient force profiles and associated flow structures to explain the aerodynamic enhancement."
